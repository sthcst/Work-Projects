*LUCKY TOKYO* is loud, layered, slightly chaotic, and impossible to 
ignore — exactly as intended. It takes the kind of visual overload that 
makes Tokyo feel alive and turns it into something you can carry every 
day on your phone, laptop, tablet, console, or whatever device in your 
life currently looks far too plain.


At the centre of it all is the *Maneki-neko* — _the lucky cat_. A small 
symbol with a big reputation. In Japanese culture, it is tied to good 
fortune, prosperity, and welcome. We liked the idea of taking that 
energy and dropping it straight onto your everyday tech. Not in a subtle 
way. In a *why-is-my-device-suddenly-this-cool* way.


But this design is not just one symbol doing all the work.

Look closer and it starts unfolding.

<https://pkr.soundestlink.com/ce/c/675ca4d10bb04571bef4b25b/69d66d892f73c84b91148ef0/69d66dd641d31987815af16e?signature=5efd3782d81e94feae8d4fc3929ebb31a762c591c5d9b99c6f2be01ab1bce2ab> 


There is the calm face of a traditional doll sitting right next to the 
drama of a kabuki mask. Koi cut through the composition like movement 
made visible. Butterflies, florals, waves, street signs, food-box 
details — every part adds to the sense that this skin did not come from 
a boardroom trying to look tasteful. It came from a love of contrast, 
clutter, symbolism, and cities that never ask permission to have 
personality.


That is what makes *LUCKY TOKYO* work.

It is busy, but not messy. Playful, but not childish. Bold, but still 
precise. The kind of design that keeps giving you something new to 
notice every time you pick up your device. A little luck here. A little 
attitude there. A lot of character everywhere else.


_We made it because not everyone wants clean and quiet._


Sometimes you want a design with pulse.
Something with stories in it.
Something that feels collected rather than decorated.
Something that looks like it has actually lived.

<https://pkr.soundestlink.com/ce/c/675ca4d10bb04571bef4b25b/69d66d892f73c84b91148ef0/69d66dd641d31987815af16f?signature=5efd3782d81e94feae8d4fc3929ebb31a762c591c5d9b99c6f2be01ab1bce2ab> 


And yes, it works across *smartphones, laptops, tablets, game consoles, 
and more* — because a design this alive should not have to stop at one 
device.


So no, we are not saying a skin can change your life.


But we are saying your device could look a lot luckier.
